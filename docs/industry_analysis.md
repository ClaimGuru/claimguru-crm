# Public Adjuster CRM Industry Analysis

## Industry Standards
Based on the competitor analysis and general search results, the following features are considered industry standards for public adjuster CRM systems:
- **Comprehensive Claims Management:** Tracking claims from initiation to settlement is the core function.
- **Document Management:** The ability to upload, store, and organize claim-related documents is essential.
- **Contact Management:** A centralized database of all contacts, including clients, insurance companies, and contractors.
- **Task Management:** Tools to create, assign, and track tasks and deadlines.
- **Client Portals:** A way for clients to log in and view the status of their claims.
- **Reporting:** Basic reporting capabilities to track key metrics.

## Emerging Technologies
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML):** This is the most significant emerging trend. AI is being used for:
    - **Predictive Analytics:** To forecast claim outcomes and identify potential issues.
    - **Automated Data Entry:** To reduce manual data entry and improve accuracy.
    - **AI-Powered Communications:** To draft emails, letters, and other communications.
    - **Document Analysis:** To extract key information from documents and identify inconsistencies.
- **Robotic Process Automation (RPA):** To automate repetitive, rules-based tasks.
- **Blockchain:** For enhanced security and transparency in the claims process.
- **Cloud Computing:** The majority of modern CRMs are cloud-based, offering accessibility and scalability.

## Pricing Benchmarks
- **Per-User, Per-Month Model:** The most common pricing model is a monthly subscription fee per user.
- **Tiered Pricing:** Most companies offer tiered pricing plans with different levels of features and user limits.
- **Price Range:** Based on the competitors analyzed, prices range from approximately **$70 to $200 per month** for a single user or small team.

## Common Integration Partners
While the analyzed competitors did not provide specific integration details, based on general industry knowledge and search results, common integrations for insurance CRMs include:
- **Email Clients:** Outlook, Gmail.
- **Accounting Software:** QuickBooks, Xero.
- **eSignature Tools:** DocuSign, HelloSign.
- **Calendaring Software:** Google Calendar, Outlook Calendar.
- **Phone Systems:** VoIP providers.
- **Third-Party Data Providers:** Weather data, property data, etc.
