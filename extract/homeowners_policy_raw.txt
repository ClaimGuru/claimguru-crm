Your insurance company must send you a notice that they are not renewing your policy. If your insurance company does not want to cancel or nonrenew your policy, but wants to make certain material changes, then they must explain the changes in writing at least 30 days before the renewal date. Instead of a notice of “material change” a company may choose to not renew your existing policy. If so, the company has to send a nonrenewal letter, but may still offer you a different policy.
